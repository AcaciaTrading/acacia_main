The various indicators and settings in the control panel can be hard to understand at first. This section provides explanations and tips to help you get started.

General Settings

Moving Average Crossover

This strategy uses 2 different moving averages. Once the shorter moving average is a specified percentage above the longer moving average, a buy order is triggered. When the shorter is a specific percentage below than the longer average, a sell order is triggered.

Different Moving Averages

EMA (Exponential Moving Average)

An Exponential Moving Average weights the most recent values more than the past ones over a given span of time. The amount of weight each price holds decreases exponentially.

SMA (Simple Moving Average)

A Simple Moving Average is the average of all of the prices in a given time span, with all of the values weighted equally.

TMA (Triangular Moving Average)

A Triangular Moving Average is a weighted average of SMA values for a given time span.

WMA (Weighted Moving Average)

An Weighted Moving Average weights the most recent values more than the past ones over a given span of time. The amount of weight each price holds decreases linearly.

Advanced Settings

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

The MACD is calculated by subtracting the 26-interval exponential moving average (EMA) from the 12-interval EMA. A nine-interval EMA of the MACD, called the "signal line", is then plotted on top of the MACD, functioning as a trigger for buy and sell signals.

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI oscillates between 0 and 100. RSI can be used to identify a general trend. According to J. Welles Wilder, the developer of the indicator, an RSI of above 70 generally indicates that something is overbought, and an RSI of below 30 indicates that something has been oversold.RSI oscillates between 0 and 100. ROC (Rate of Change)

The rate of change is essentially the slope of the price of Bitcoin throughout a time span of 14 intervals. This strategy works with values that are both positive and negative because it is based on the slope.
